## Legal Considerations

### 1. Data Privacy Laws

#### China - Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL)
- Requires consent for collecting personal information
- Purpose limitation principle
- Data minimization requirement
- **Penalties**: Up to 50 million RMB or 5% of annual revenue

#### European Union - GDPR
- Personal data includes phone numbers
- Requires lawful basis for processing
- Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
- **Penalties**: Up to €20 million or 4% of global revenue

#### United States - State Laws
- **CCPA/CPRA** (California): Requires disclosure of data collection
- **TCPA**: Restricts unsolicited calls/messages to mobile numbers
- **CAN-SPAM**: Regulates commercial email

### 2. Website Terms of Service

Most websites prohibit:
- Automated data collection without permission
- Scraping of user-generated content
- Commercial use of scraped data
- Circumventing anti-bot measures

**Violations can result in:**
- Legal action (CFAA in US)
- IP blocking
- Account termination
- Civil liability

### 3. Criminal Laws

In many jurisdictions:
- **Harassment**: Using phone numbers for unwanted contact
- **Stalking**: Systematic collection of personal data
- **Fraud**: Using numbers for scams or identity theft
- **Cybercrime**: Unauthorized access to computer systems

## Ethical Guidelines

### ✅ Acceptable Use

1. **Security Research**
   - Testing your own systems
   - Authorized penetration testing
   - Academic research with IRB approval

2. **Legitimate Business**
   - Contacting users who explicitly opted in
   - Customer service (your own customers)
   - Compliance monitoring (your own content)

3. **Public Interest**
   - Journalism (with editorial oversight)
   - Public safety research
   - whistleblower reporting

### ❌ Prohibited Use

1. **Spam & Unsolicited Contact**
   - SMS marketing without consent
   - Telemarketing cold calls
   - WhatsApp/Telegram spam

2. **Harassment**
   - Doxxing
   - Coordinated harassment
   - Threatening communications

3. **Fraud**
   - Phishing attacks
   - Social engineering
   - Identity theft

4. **Commercial Exploitation**
   - Selling contact lists
   - Data brokerage
   - Unauthorized marketing

## Best Practices

### Before Using This Tool

1. **Check Legal Authority**
   - Consult with legal counsel
   - Review relevant laws in your jurisdiction
   - Understand target website's ToS

2. **Minimize Data Collection**
   - Only collect what you need
   - Don't store data longer than necessary
   - Anonymize where possible

3. **Obtain Consent**
   - Prefer opt-in approaches
   - Provide clear privacy notices
   - Honor opt-out requests

4. **Secure the Data**
   - Encrypt stored data
   - Limit access
   - Regular security audits
